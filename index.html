The problem

You are running a dozen marketing activities at once. Content, ads, outbound, events, a redesign. Money and time leave every month. But when someone asks which of it is actually creating pipeline, the honest answer is a guess.

You can't say which channel brings your best customers, only which ones are busy.

Your reporting shows activity, not pipeline. Clicks and impressions, not revenue.

Every new idea feels like a bet, not a decision you can defend with evidence.

Why it happens

Why most SaaS marketing quietly underperforms.

It is rarely a lack of effort. It is almost always one of these three.

01

Tactics before positioning

Teams reach for channels before they are clear on who they are for and why it matters. Even sharp targeting cannot rescue a message that does not land.

02

Spending without real attribution

When tracking is broken or missing, every budget call is made half-blind. Most accounts have gaps here, and it quietly distorts every decision downstream.

03

Activity mistaken for progress

Dashboards full of impressions and clicks feel productive. They rarely answer the only question that matters: is any of this reaching pipeline.
